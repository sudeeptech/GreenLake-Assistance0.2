About Service Subscriptions — HPE GreenLake

Service subscriptions allow users to activate and use services within a workspace.
A subscription key is required to enable a service.

Only one subscription is needed per service and workspace.
When a service is deployed, the subscription is automatically applied.

Prerequisites for Adding Service Subscription

User must have Workspace Administrator role OR permission to modify subscriptions.

Services must be set up before adding a subscription.

Subscription key must be received from HPE via email.

Steps to Add Service Subscription
Step 1 — Open Service Subscription Page

Login to GreenLake Cloud.

Click Global Navigation Menu.

Go to:

Services → Manage Service Subscription.

Click Add Service Subscription.

Step 2 — Enter Subscription Key

Enter the subscription key received via email.

Click Next.

Step 3 — Assign Tags (Optional)

Select or create tag name.

Select or create tag value.

Click Assign.

Maximum 50 tags allowed.

Click Next.

Step 4 — Manage Showback Rates (Optional)

Used only for usage monitoring services.

Can skip this step.

Enter effective date and rate if required.

Click Next.

Step 5 — Review and Finish

Verify all details.

Click Finish.

Service subscription is added to workspace.

Viewing Service Subscriptions

Go to Services → Manage Service Subscription.

View:

Subscription status

Expiration date

Subscription tier

Subscription type.

Removing Service Subscription from Workspace
Prerequisites

Workspace Administrator role required.

Steps

Go to Services → Manage Service Subscription.

Search for subscription.

Select subscription.

Click Actions → Remove Subscriptions.

Confirm removal.

After removal:

Subscription can be added to another workspace.

Tags are removed.

Action recorded in audit log.

Managing Tags for Service Subscriptions

Users can create, assign, or remove tags.

Steps

Go to Services → Manage Service Subscription.

Select subscription.

Click Actions → Manage Tags.

Add or remove tags.

Click Save Changes.

Maximum 50 tags allowed.

Viewing and Downloading Subscription Data

Users can export subscription details.

Steps

Go to Services → Manage Service Subscription.

Apply filters if required.

Click Actions → Export.

Enter email address.

Generate report.

Download report from email.

Report contains:

Subscription tier

Status

Expiration date

Subscription type.

Adding Services to Workspace (Service Deployment)

Service subscription works only after service deployment.

Steps

Go to Services → Service Catalog.

Select service.

Click Launch.

Follow Deploy Service wizard.

If user lacks permission → contact administrator.

Important Notes

Subscription is automatically applied when service is deployed.

Only one subscription per service per workspace is required.

Subscription may take time to provision.

If subscription exists in another workspace, remove it before adding.

Enabling the MSP mode

Procedure
Log in to your workspace as an administrator.
On the GreenLake cloud header, click the workspace menu and then select Manage Workspace.
Click Manage Workspace Type.
The Manage workspace type page displays the types of workspaces supported by GreenLake and the Requirements to convert to a managed service provider workspace.
There are four steps required to enable a managed service provider workspace.
Step 1 - Check your eligibility

Step 2 - Remove assignments and subscriptions from All Devices

Step 3 - Remove all unsupported services

Step 4 - Convert your workspace

Click the Check Eligibility button.
The Provide Workspace Details page appears where you can enter additional workspace information to determine if you can convert to a Managed Service Provider workspace.
Provide the workspace details using the check boxes, fields, and drop-downs options. This is step one of a two-step process. Click Submit when you are done.
After your eligibility is checked, a message appears indicating that you are eligible to convert to a Manage Service Provider workspace.
Click Continue.
NOTE
Before you can convert to a Managed Service Provider workspace, you must complete the remaining steps in the conversion process.
Complete Step 2 of the required steps to continue: Remove assignments and subscriptions from all devices. Click the Review Unsupported Service Managers button.
If you have unsupported services, they are displayed on the next page.
Agree to the terms about the removal of unsupported service instances.
Click the Remove All Unsupported Services button.
This completes Step 3 and displays the Manage Workspace Type page. You can now convert your workspace.
Click the Convert to MSP mode button, Step 4. The Convert Your workspace message displays notifying you that your workspace will be unavailable during conversion, and you will be temporarily logged out.
Click the Yes, Convert Now button.
You are logged out of your current workspace and automatically redirected to the login page.
Navigate to the workspace management page and click the Menu icon.
The Global navigation menu global navigation menu icon in the GreenLake cloud header now contains a Tenants option.

Disabling the MSP mode

Log in to your workspace as an administrator.
On the Manage Workspace page, select the Manage Workspace Type link on your workspace card.
Under the Requirements for a Standard Enterprise Workspace section, click Review Tenant Workspaces.
Review and delete the tenant workspaces.
Click the Convert to Standard Enterprise Mode button under the Requirements for a Standard Enterprises Workspace section.
Click the Yes, Convert Now button.
You are logged out of your current workspace and automatically redirected to the GreenLake login page.

Password Reset Guide:

Step 1: Go to the GreenLake workspace login page: https://greenlake.cloud/login
Step 2: Click on "Forgot Password".
Step 3: Enter your registered email address.
Step 4: Check your email and click the reset link.
Step 5: Enter a new password that meets the following requirements:
   - At least 12 characters long
   - Contains at least one uppercase letter
   - Contains at least one lowercase letter
   - Contains at least one number
   - Contains at least one special character (e.g., !@#$%^&*)
Step 6: Confirm your new password and click "Submit".
Step 7: Log in with your new password.

Inviting users
About this task
Account administrators can easily invite team members to join GreenLake cloud by sending them a sign-up link in an email using either of the following options:
The Invite users card on the Quick Actions section of the Workspace identity & access page

The Invite Users button on the Workspace identity & access > Users page

NOTE
In an MSP environment, user access and privileges are based on the workspace from which the invite is issued. Users invited from a tenant workspace are tenant users and users invited from the MSP workspace are MSP users.
Procedure
On the GreenLake cloud header, click the workspace menu and then select Manage Workspace.
On the Manage Workspace page, click the Workspace identity & access card.
On the Workspace identity & access page, click the Invite Users card.
The Invite User window opens.

Deleting a user
Procedure
On the GreenLake cloud header, click the workspace menu and then select Manage Workspace.
Click the Workspace identity & access card and then on the Workspace identity & access page, click the Users card.
The Users page displays a list of verified and unverified users.
Select the check box next to the name of a user and click the ellipses (…) and then select Delete.
Confirm the user deletion in the Delete User action dialog.


Viewing audit logs for users
About this task
Audit logs are generated when a new user is created and an existing user is modified or deleted from the GreenLake cloud workspace. It also records the login and logout activities of users.

Procedure
On the GreenLake cloud header, click the workspace menu and then select Manage Workspace.
On the Manage Workspace page, click the Audit Log card.
The Audit Log page appears.
To filter audit logs about user activity, use the filter or click the Actions > Edit Columns link, and select up to six different columns.
To sort audit logs by user name, click the Username column heading.

Creating user roles
About this task
You can create custom roles for specific services on the GreenLake cloud and add permissions to define the access privileges for users assigned the role.
Procedure
On the GreenLake cloud header, click the workspace menu and then select Manage Workspace.
On the Manage Workspace page, click the Workspace identity & access card.
On the Workspace identity & access page, click the Roles & Permissions card.
The Roles & Permissions page appears.
Click the Create role button.
NOTE
Use the Duplicate existing role option to make a copy of an existing custom role and modify it to suit your needs. Built-in or predefined roles cannot be duplicated or edited.
From the Service drop-down list, select the service to which the role applies.
Click Next.
The Create Role Wizard appears.
Follow the directions on the Create Role Wizard to complete the role setup.
Add Name & Description screen—add a role name and description and click Next.
Add <Service> Permissions screens—click Add Permissions to access the permissions screens where you can select the actions that a user with this role can perform in the selected service. After adding permissions, click Next to continue.
Review & Create screen—this screen shows you the role details, including the permission granted for each role. Use this screen to review your role configuration before creating it. You can change the role name and permissions using the Modify link.
Click Finish to create the role.
The new role is added to the Roles & Permissions page. Use the search or sort the list by name to find the newly created role in the list.

Viewing resource restriction policy details
Procedure
On the GreenLake cloud header, click the workspace menu and then select Manage Workspace.
On the Manage Workspace page, click the Workspace identity & access card.
On the Workspace identity & access page, click the Resource Restriction Policy card.
The Resource Restriction Policies page displays a list of resource restriction policies (RRP) and their information.
Name
The name of the RRP
Description
A brief description of the RRP
Service
The service for which the RRP defines access limits
Date Created
The date the RRP was created
The Create Resource Restriction Policy button accesses a wizard that steps you through the process for creating a new RRP.
Click an RRP to display its details.
The Details section provides basic information from the table view on the main RRP page.

The Resources section lists the resources included in the RRP. Scope resources are listed in bold with their member scope instances below them.

The Role Assignments section lists the users and the role assignments where this RRP is applied. The list displays up to ten role assignments on the page; use the page numbers to view additional roles assigned to the user.


Adding tags to devices using a CSV file
About this task
You can manually assign tags to devices once you have added the devices to your workspace. Alternatively, you can assign tags to networking and compute devices using a CSV file when you add these devices to your workspace.
Procedure
On the GreenLake cloud header, click the Global navigation menu global navigation menu icon.
Select Devices > Devices Inventory.
The Inventory page opens showing all devices in your inventory.
Click Add Devices to add devices to your inventory.
Select the Device Type from the drop-down menu.
On the Upload CSV File page, provide details about each device you want to add, including the serial number and other identifying information. The details required vary according to the device type.
You can add each device individually but, if you want to add multiple networking or compute devices to your workspace, use a CSV file. You can also assign tags to each device you include in the file.
To add devices using a CSV file, select .CSV File for the Ownership Type.
Click Download Sample File and review the device information required.
Edit the sample file to add details for each of the devices you want to add. You can also specify tags for each device.

Assigning devices to a service
About this task
The Inventory section on the Devices page enables you to assign, update, or even remove the subscription of a device from a service instance.

Procedure
On the GreenLake cloud header, click the Global navigation menu global navigation menu icon.
Select Devices > Devices Inventory.
The Inventory page opens showing all devices in your inventory.
Click the Require Service Assignments card.
The inventory list displays all devices requiring assignments.
Use the check boxes to select the devices you want to assign to a service.
From the Actions drop-down, click Assign to Service.
If you have multiple workspaces, the Select a Workspace page appears with the list of your existing workspaces.
Select a workspace and click Next.
On the Assign Devices to Service Instance page, from the drop-down lists, select a Service instance and a Region (for the service instance) to assign your devices.
A Devices Assigned dialog appears.
Click View Audit Log to check the audit logs for assignment activity details, otherwise, click Close.
Click Finish.

Removing device subscriptions from services
Prerequisites
You have a workspace administrator role or a role with permissions to modify subscriptions.

About this task
You can remove the subscription of a device from a service instance at any time.
NOTE
Removing assignments automatically removes the subscriptions for the devices.
Procedure
On the GreenLake cloud header, click the Global navigation menu global navigation menu icon.
Select Devices > Devices Inventory.
The Inventory page opens showing all devices in your inventory.
Click the Assigned & Subscribed card to list all devices that are assigned and subscribed.
Click the check boxes to select the devices that you want to unsubscribe.
From the Actions drop-down, select Remove Assignment.
On the Remove Assignment dialog box, click Remove service Assignments to confirm the removal of the assignments.
